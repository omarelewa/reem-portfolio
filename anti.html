01 — The brief

The anti-aging aisle is a wall of clinical white bottles making promises no one believes. anti: had to feel like the opposite — warm, honest, and human.

I wanted to turn "fighting aging" from something anxious into something tender — a daily ritual, not a war against your own face. The brief boiled down to one idea sturdy enough to carry a name, a logo, four product lines and a campaign.

02 — The idea

If aging is a race against time, make the whole brand about time itself.

So the identity runs on two symbols of time: the hourglass, and the golden hour.

The name says it plainly — anti: — with a colon that turns every product into a statement: anti-UV, anti-dry, anti-trace, anti-time. The colon became the logo's fingerprint. The palette stays in the sunset: amber, terracotta, cream.
