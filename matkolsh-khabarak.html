The habit

In Egypt, old newspapers end their lives under food. Bread comes home wrapped in yesterday’s news, and some of the ink always stays on the crust.

The name is a pun — متكلش خبرك means don’t eat your news, one letter away from don’t eat your bread. I turned the habit into a small magazine: real trays, real carts, real loaves, photographed the way you actually find them.

The flag

A masthead you swing, not set.

The title is calligraphy, swung across the corner of every cover like a signature — the same stroke you’d find on the carts the newspapers ride in. Everything else keeps newspaper manners: justified columns, a dateline running up the edge, one red box for the story.
